# A Phase 3 Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Recanumab in Early Symptomatic Alzheimer's Disease

**Protocol Number:** NEURO-2026-019
**Sponsor:** Helix Neurosciences
**IND Number:** 168,977
**Phase:** Phase 3
**Indication:** Early symptomatic Alzheimer's disease (mild cognitive impairment due to AD or mild AD dementia)
**Intervention:** Recanumab 10 mg/kg IV every 4 weeks for 76 weeks versus placebo IV at the same schedule.

## 1. Background

Recanumab is a humanized monoclonal antibody targeting aggregated amyloid-beta protofibrils. Building on the recent class of anti-amyloid therapies, recanumab aims to slow cognitive and functional decline in early AD.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** Demonstrate slowing of cognitive and functional decline.

**Primary Endpoint:** Change from baseline in Clinical Dementia Rating-Sum of Boxes (CDR-SB) at week 76.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- Change in ADAS-Cog13
- ADCS-ADL-MCI
- Change in brain amyloid by PET (Centiloid scale)

## 3. Study Design

Multicenter, randomized 1:1, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group trial. Subjects (N=1736) will be stratified by APOE ε4 status and use of symptomatic AD medications.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Adults aged 50-85; MCI due to AD or mild AD dementia; positive amyloid PET or CSF biomarker; MMSE 22-30; CDR global 0.5 or 1.

**Exclusion:** Brain MRI showing >4 cerebral microhemorrhages, any macrohemorrhage, or superficial siderosis; concurrent anticoagulation other than aspirin ≤325 mg.

## 5. Adverse Events

Amyloid-related class effects warrant intensive MRI surveillance:

- **ARIA-E (amyloid-related imaging abnormalities, edema)** — 17% of treated subjects; majority asymptomatic; symptomatic ARIA-E in 4%.
- **ARIA-H (microhemorrhage or superficial siderosis)** — 14%, predominantly asymptomatic.
- **Infusion reaction** — 22%, mild to moderate; tachycardia and hypotension reported.
- **Headache** — 16%, mild to moderate.
- **Fatigue** — 9%.
- **Nausea** — 5%.
- **Dizziness** — 7%.
- **Falls** — 8% (more common in subjects with prior ARIA).
- **Confusion** — 4%, may reflect symptomatic ARIA-E.

MRI required at screening and weeks 5, 13, 25, 37, 53, and 77, plus any time symptomatic ARIA suspected. APOE ε4 homozygotes carry highest ARIA risk and will undergo additional consenting.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

N=1736 provides 90% power to detect a 25% slowing in CDR-SB decline at week 76, assuming a 1.5-point progression in placebo over 18 months.

## 7. Monitoring

Independent neuroradiology adjudication of all MRIs. Specialized ARIA management algorithm included as Appendix B.
