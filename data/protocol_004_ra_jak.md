# A Phase 2b Randomized, Double-Blind, Active-Comparator Trial of Selacitinib in Patients With Moderate-to-Severe Rheumatoid Arthritis With Inadequate Response to Methotrexate

**Protocol Number:** RA-2026-003
**Sponsor:** Glenwood Biopharma
**IND Number:** 188,210
**Phase:** Phase 2b
**Indication:** Moderate-to-severe rheumatoid arthritis
**Intervention:** Selacitinib 7 mg or 15 mg orally twice daily versus adalimumab 40 mg subcutaneous every 2 weeks, for 24 weeks, on background methotrexate.

## 1. Background

Janus kinase (JAK) inhibitors have transformed RA management for methotrexate-inadequate responders. Selacitinib is a selective JAK1 inhibitor with reduced JAK2/JAK3 activity, designed to mitigate hematologic and cardiovascular safety concerns observed with pan-JAK inhibitors.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** To compare ACR20 response rates at week 12 between selacitinib doses and adalimumab.

**Primary Endpoint:** Proportion of subjects achieving ACR20 response at week 12.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- ACR50, ACR70 at weeks 12 and 24
- DAS28-CRP remission
- Health Assessment Questionnaire Disability Index (HAQ-DI)

## 3. Study Design

Three-arm, randomized (1:1:1), double-blind, double-dummy, parallel-group, active-controlled trial. Subjects (N=486) on stable methotrexate.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Adults aged 18-75; RA diagnosis per 2010 ACR/EULAR criteria for ≥6 months; ≥6 swollen and ≥6 tender joints; CRP ≥1.5 × ULN; inadequate response to methotrexate after ≥3 months at ≥15 mg/week.

**Exclusion:** Prior biologic or targeted synthetic DMARD; active or latent untreated tuberculosis; recent serious infection.

## 5. Adverse Events

JAK inhibitor class effects warrant active monitoring:

- **Upper respiratory infection** — 18%, mild.
- **Herpes zoster** — 4%, mostly Grade 1-2.
- **Headache** — 12%, mild.
- **Nausea** — 9%, mild.
- **Diarrhea** — 6%.
- **Anemia** — 8%, Grade 2.
- **Neutropenia** — 5%, Grade 2-3.
- **Lymphopenia** — 9%, Grade 2.
- **Elevated ALT** — 7%, transaminitis typically resolving on dose hold.
- **Elevated AST** — 5%.
- **Hypertension** — 4%, mild to moderate.
- **Injection site reaction (adalimumab arm)** — 6%.
- **Serious infection** — 2.1% per patient-year.
- **Major adverse cardiovascular event (MACE)** — adjudicated; expected <0.5%.

All subjects will undergo TB screening prior to enrollment. Lipid panels at weeks 4, 12, and 24.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

Sample size of N=486 provides 90% power to demonstrate non-inferiority of selacitinib 15 mg vs adalimumab on ACR20 at week 12 with a margin of 12%.

## 7. Monitoring

Independent DSMB with cardiovascular and infection subcommittees.
