# A Phase 3 Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Psilocynex in Adults With Treatment-Resistant Major Depressive Disorder

**Protocol Number:** PSY-2026-011
**Sponsor:** Lumen Mental Health Therapeutics
**IND Number:** 175,442
**Phase:** Phase 3
**Indication:** Treatment-resistant major depressive disorder (MDD)
**Intervention:** Single 25 mg oral dose of psilocynex (synthetic psilocybin analogue) administered in a supervised setting, with psychological support.

## 1. Background

Psilocybin-based therapies have shown rapid antidepressant effects in Phase 2 trials. Psilocynex is a synthetic analogue with improved pharmacokinetic predictability. Treatment-resistant depression remains a significant unmet need.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** Assess the antidepressant efficacy of a single dose of psilocynex 25 mg versus placebo.

**Primary Endpoint:** Change from baseline in MADRS total score at day 43.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- MADRS response and remission rates
- Sustained response at week 12
- Patient Global Impression of Change

## 3. Study Design

Multi-site, randomized 2:1, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. Subjects (N=234) will receive a single supervised dose, with structured pre- and post-dose psychological support sessions.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Adults aged 18-65; MDD per DSM-5; current depressive episode duration ≥3 months; failure of ≥2 adequate antidepressant trials; MADRS ≥28.

**Exclusion:** Personal or first-degree family history of psychotic disorder or bipolar I disorder; active suicidal ideation with intent; significant cardiovascular disease.

## 5. Adverse Events

Adverse events anticipated within 24 hours post-dose, mostly transient:

- **Headache** — 35%, mild to moderate, peaks 2-6 hours post-dose.
- **Nausea** — 28%, mild.
- **Dizziness** — 14%, mild.
- **Anxiety** — 12% during the acute period; moderate.
- **Fatigue** — 18%, mild.
- **Insomnia** — 9% on dosing night.
- **Hypertension** — transient elevation in 11%, mild.
- **Tachycardia** — transient, 8%.
- **Visual perceptual changes** — 92% during acute period, anticipated pharmacological effect.
- **Suicidal ideation** — monitored per C-SSRS; emergent ideation in 1.5% of treated subjects (vs 1.0% placebo).

A trained therapist will remain present throughout dosing and for at least 6 hours post-dose. Subjects discharged only after cardiovascular and psychological stability confirmed.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

N=234 provides 90% power to detect a 6.5-point MADRS difference at day 43 between treatment and placebo arms (two-sided alpha 0.05).

## 7. Monitoring

Independent psychiatric DSMB; mandatory C-SSRS reporting after every visit through week 12.
