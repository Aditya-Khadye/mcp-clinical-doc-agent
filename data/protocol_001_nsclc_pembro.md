# A Phase 3, Randomized, Double-Blind Study of Pembrolizumab Plus Platinum-Doublet Chemotherapy Versus Placebo Plus Platinum-Doublet Chemotherapy in Patients With Previously Untreated Stage IV Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

**Protocol Number:** ONC-2026-014
**Sponsor:** Aurora Oncology Research
**IND Number:** 178,452
**Phase:** Phase 3
**Indication:** Stage IV non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC)
**Intervention:** Pembrolizumab 200 mg IV every 3 weeks in combination with carboplatin AUC 5 and pemetrexed 500 mg/m² IV every 3 weeks for 4 cycles, followed by pembrolizumab plus pemetrexed maintenance.

## 1. Background and Rationale

Lung cancer remains the leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide. Non-small cell lung cancer accounts for approximately 84% of all lung cancer diagnoses. While checkpoint inhibitors targeting PD-1 have improved survival outcomes for patients with metastatic NSCLC, combination strategies with platinum-based chemotherapy may further enhance efficacy in PD-L1 low and negative tumors.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** To compare overall survival (OS) between the pembrolizumab combination arm and the placebo combination arm.

**Primary Endpoint:** Overall survival, defined as the time from randomization to death from any cause.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- Progression-free survival per RECIST v1.1
- Objective response rate
- Duration of response
- Patient-reported quality of life (EORTC QLQ-C30)

## 3. Study Design

A multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. Subjects (N=620) will be randomized 1:1 to receive either pembrolizumab plus chemotherapy or placebo plus chemotherapy. Randomization will be stratified by PD-L1 expression, ECOG performance status, and histology.

## 4. Eligibility Criteria

**Inclusion:** Adults ≥18 years of age; histologically or cytologically confirmed Stage IV NSCLC; ECOG performance status 0-1; measurable disease per RECIST v1.1; adequate organ function.

**Exclusion:** Prior systemic therapy for metastatic NSCLC; known EGFR, ALK, or ROS1 alterations; active autoimmune disease requiring systemic therapy within the past 2 years.

## 5. Adverse Events and Safety

Based on prior pembrolizumab combination studies, the following adverse events are anticipated. Investigators will use CTCAE v5.0 grading.

- **Fatigue** — observed in approximately 38% of patients; typically Grade 1-2.
- **Nausea** — 32%, generally mild to moderate.
- **Anemia** — 28%, Grade 2-3 in 12% of patients.
- **Neutropenia** — 22%, with Grade 3 events occurring in 9%.
- **Diarrhea** — 19%, moderate severity.
- **Rash** — 14%, frequently immune-related; pruritus reported in 8%.
- **Pneumonitis** — 6%, immune-related; one Grade 3 case anticipated per 100 patient-years.
- **Hepatotoxicity** — elevated ALT in 7%, elevated AST in 6%.
- **Hypothyroidism** — 11%.

Serious adverse events of special interest include immune-mediated pneumonitis, colitis, and hepatitis. All Grade 3 or higher events will trigger expedited reporting per FDA 21 CFR 312.32.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

A sample size of N=620 provides 90% power to detect a hazard ratio of 0.70 for overall survival, assuming a median OS of 13 months in the control arm, two-sided alpha of 0.05, and 24 months of accrual followed by 18 months of follow-up.

## 7. Data Monitoring

An independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) will review unblinded safety data every 6 months.
