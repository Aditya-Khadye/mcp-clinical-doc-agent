# A Phase 3 Open-Label Extension Study of Once-Weekly Tirzeglutide in Adults With Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus Inadequately Controlled on Metformin

**Protocol Number:** ENDO-2026-022
**Sponsor:** Pinewood Endocrine Sciences
**IND Number:** 198,733
**Phase:** Phase 3
**Indication:** Type 2 diabetes mellitus
**Intervention:** Tirzeglutide 5 mg, 10 mg, or 15 mg subcutaneous injection once weekly for 52 weeks.

## 1. Background and Rationale

Tirzeglutide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. Building on the prior 26-week registration trial, this extension evaluates long-term glycemic control, weight loss durability, and safety.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** To evaluate change in HbA1c from baseline through week 52.

**Primary Endpoint:** Change from baseline in HbA1c at week 52.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- Proportion of subjects achieving HbA1c <7.0%
- Change in body weight
- Change in fasting plasma glucose

## 3. Study Design

Open-label, three-arm, parallel-group extension trial. Subjects (N=864) who completed the lead-in study and remained eligible will be re-randomized 1:1:1 to one of three tirzeglutide dose levels.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Adults aged 18-75 with type 2 diabetes; HbA1c 7.5-10.5%; on stable metformin ≥1500 mg/day for ≥3 months; BMI ≥25 kg/m².

**Exclusion:** History of pancreatitis; personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma; severe gastroparesis.

## 5. Adverse Events

Anticipated gastrointestinal and metabolic events typical of incretin therapy:

- **Nausea** — 28% at 5 mg dose, up to 44% at 15 mg dose; predominantly mild and transient during dose escalation.
- **Vomiting** — 14%, moderate; usually resolves within 4 weeks.
- **Diarrhea** — 19%, mild.
- **Constipation** — 8%, mild.
- **Dyspepsia** — 7%, mild.
- **Abdominal pain** — 6%.
- **Decreased appetite** — 16%; an anticipated pharmacological effect.
- **Injection site reaction** — 3%, mild.
- **Hypoglycemia** — 4% (more common when combined with sulfonylureas, up to 18%).
- **Headache** — 9%, mild to moderate.
- **Fatigue** — 11%.
- **Gallbladder events (cholelithiasis, cholecystitis)** — 1.2%, moderate to severe.

Pancreatitis events will undergo independent adjudication. Calcitonin will be measured at screening, week 26, and end-of-study.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

Sample size of N=864 provides >95% power to detect a 0.4% difference in HbA1c reduction between dose arms.

## 7. Monitoring

DSMB review every 6 months. Pre-specified safety stopping rules for confirmed pancreatitis (>1% incidence).
