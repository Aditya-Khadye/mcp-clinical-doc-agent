# A Phase 2 Open-Label, Multiple-Ascending-Dose Study of Mirenosense, an Antisense Oligonucleotide, in Adults With SOD1-Associated Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis

**Protocol Number:** ALS-2026-002
**Sponsor:** Arrowhead Neurogenetics
**IND Number:** 181,224
**Phase:** Phase 2
**Indication:** SOD1-associated amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS)
**Intervention:** Mirenosense intrathecal injection at escalating dose levels (20 mg, 40 mg, 60 mg), administered every 4 weeks for 24 weeks.

## 1. Background

Approximately 2-3% of ALS cases are linked to pathogenic variants in SOD1. Mirenosense is an antisense oligonucleotide designed to reduce SOD1 mRNA expression in motor neurons. Early data suggest CSF neurofilament reduction may predict clinical benefit.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** Characterize safety, tolerability, and pharmacodynamics across ascending dose levels.

**Primary Endpoint:** Incidence of treatment-emergent adverse events through week 28.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- Change in CSF SOD1 protein
- Change in serum neurofilament light chain (NfL)
- ALSFRS-R total score

## 3. Study Design

Open-label, sequential dose-escalation trial. Three cohorts of N=18 (54 total subjects), with each cohort proceeding only after independent safety review of the prior cohort.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Adults aged 18-75; diagnosis of ALS per revised El Escorial criteria; confirmed pathogenic SOD1 variant; ALSFRS-R ≥30; SVC ≥60% predicted.

**Exclusion:** Tracheostomy or invasive ventilation; contraindication to lumbar puncture; recent use of investigational ALS therapy.

## 5. Adverse Events

Anticipated procedural and drug-related events:

- **Headache** — 38%, mild to moderate (post-LP and drug-related); typically resolves within 72 hours.
- **Back pain** — 27%, mild, related to intrathecal administration.
- **Nausea** — 11%, mild.
- **Fatigue** — 22%, mild to moderate.
- **Fever** — 9%, mild.
- **Elevated CSF protein** — 18%, generally asymptomatic.
- **Lymphocytic pleocytosis (CSF)** — 12%, mild.
- **Thrombocytopenia** — 4%, Grade 1-2.
- **Hepatotoxicity (elevated ALT)** — 6%, Grade 1-2.
- **Injection site pain** — 14%, mild.
- **Aseptic meningitis** — 1.8%, moderate; managed with dose interruption.

Each intrathecal dose administered by a trained neurologist or interventional radiologist. CSF and serum biomarker collection at each dosing visit.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

Sample size of N=54 supports characterization of safety with 80% probability of observing any AE with true incidence ≥3%.

## 7. Monitoring

Internal safety monitoring committee with neurology and clinical pharmacology expertise. Stopping rules for confirmed Grade 4 events or aseptic meningitis rate >10%.
