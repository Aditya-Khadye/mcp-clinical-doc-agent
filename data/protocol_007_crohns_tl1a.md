# A Phase 2 Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Induction Study of Tulemumab in Adults With Moderately to Severely Active Crohn's Disease

**Protocol Number:** GI-2026-005
**Sponsor:** Cobalt Gastroenterology Research
**IND Number:** 184,560
**Phase:** Phase 2
**Indication:** Moderately to severely active Crohn's disease
**Intervention:** Tulemumab 600 mg IV at weeks 0, 4, and 8 versus placebo IV at the same intervals.

## 1. Background

TL1A (TNF-like ligand 1A) signaling drives intestinal inflammation and fibrosis. Tulemumab is a humanized IgG1 monoclonal antibody neutralizing TL1A. Prior Phase 1 data demonstrated favorable pharmacokinetics with a 21-day half-life.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** To evaluate clinical remission of Crohn's disease following induction therapy with tulemumab.

**Primary Endpoint:** Proportion of subjects achieving clinical remission (CDAI <150) at week 12.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- Endoscopic response (SES-CD reduction ≥50%)
- CRP and fecal calprotectin normalization
- IBDQ score improvement

## 3. Study Design

Randomized 1:1, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group, multicenter induction trial. Subjects (N=178) stratified by prior biologic exposure and disease location.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Adults aged 18-75; Crohn's disease confirmed by endoscopy; CDAI 220-450; CRP ≥3 mg/L or fecal calprotectin ≥250 µg/g; prior failure or intolerance to ≥1 conventional therapy.

**Exclusion:** Active intra-abdominal abscess; symptomatic stenosis requiring intervention; recent TNF antagonist exposure within 12 weeks.

## 5. Adverse Events

Expected safety profile:

- **Upper respiratory infection** — 11%, mild.
- **Headache** — 9%, mild.
- **Nausea** — 7%, mild.
- **Diarrhea** — 8% (may reflect underlying disease activity).
- **Abdominal pain** — 12% (also disease-related).
- **Infusion reaction** — 4%, mild to moderate; one Grade 3 anticipated per 100 infusions.
- **Anemia** — 6%, mild.
- **Elevated ALT** — 4%, transaminitis typically resolving without intervention.
- **Pneumonia** — 1.2%, moderate.
- **Herpes zoster** — 0.8%.
- **Injection site reaction (subcutaneous comparator if added)** — N/A in this trial.

Pre-treatment TB screening required. Hepatitis B and C screening mandatory.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

N=178 provides 80% power to detect a 22 percentage-point difference in remission rate (35% tulemumab vs 13% placebo).

## 7. Monitoring

DSMB review every 8 weeks during the induction period.
