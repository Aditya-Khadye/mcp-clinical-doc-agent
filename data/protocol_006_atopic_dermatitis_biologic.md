# A Phase 3 Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Nemoluzumab in Adults With Moderate-to-Severe Atopic Dermatitis

**Protocol Number:** DERM-2026-008
**Sponsor:** Vesper Dermatology Inc.
**IND Number:** 192,118
**Phase:** Phase 3
**Indication:** Moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis
**Intervention:** Nemoluzumab 300 mg subcutaneous every 4 weeks for 52 weeks versus placebo, with rescue topical therapy permitted from week 16.

## 1. Background

Nemoluzumab is a monoclonal antibody targeting IL-31 receptor A, blocking pruritus signaling and downstream inflammatory cascades. Prior Phase 2b data demonstrated rapid pruritus reduction within 1 week.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** To assess the efficacy of nemoluzumab on disease severity and pruritus at week 16.

**Primary Endpoint:** Proportion of subjects achieving IGA score of 0 or 1 with ≥2-point reduction at week 16.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- EASI-75 response at week 16 and 52
- ≥4-point reduction in Peak Pruritus NRS
- DLQI improvement

## 3. Study Design

Randomized 2:1 (nemoluzumab : placebo), double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group trial. Subjects (N=540) stratified by baseline IGA and geographic region.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Adults aged 18-75; atopic dermatitis ≥3 years; IGA ≥3; EASI ≥16; BSA ≥10%; documented inadequate response to topical therapy.

**Exclusion:** Concurrent skin condition that would interfere with assessment; recent biologic therapy within 5 half-lives; active infection requiring systemic therapy.

## 5. Adverse Events

Expected safety based on Phase 2b data:

- **Injection site reaction** — 9%, mild to moderate.
- **Upper respiratory infection** — 12%, mild.
- **Headache** — 8%, mild.
- **Nasopharyngitis** — 14%, mild.
- **Rash** — 5% (paradoxical), mild.
- **Pruritus flare** — 3%.
- **Conjunctivitis** — 4%, mild to moderate; consistent with IL-13 axis biologics.
- **Eczema herpeticum** — 1.1%, moderate to severe; warrants discontinuation.
- **Hypersensitivity reactions** — 1.5%, predominantly mild urticaria.
- **Elevated AST/ALT** — <2%.

Subjects with active conjunctivitis at screening will be excluded. Ophthalmologic monitoring at weeks 4, 16, 28, and 52.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

N=540 provides >99% power to detect a 25 percentage-point difference in IGA 0/1 response between nemoluzumab and placebo at week 16.

## 7. Monitoring

DSMB with dermatology and ophthalmology expertise. Pre-specified safety review at week 16 enrollment milestone.
