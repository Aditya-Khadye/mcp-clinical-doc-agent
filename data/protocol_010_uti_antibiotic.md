# A Phase 3 Randomized, Double-Blind, Active-Comparator Trial of Cefronexine Versus Ceftriaxone for Complicated Urinary Tract Infection in Hospitalized Adults

**Protocol Number:** ID-2026-013
**Sponsor:** Westwood Anti-Infectives
**IND Number:** 196,002
**Phase:** Phase 3
**Indication:** Complicated urinary tract infection (cUTI), including acute pyelonephritis
**Intervention:** Cefronexine 2 g IV every 12 hours for 7-14 days versus ceftriaxone 1 g IV every 24 hours for the same duration.

## 1. Background

Increasing resistance among Enterobacterales necessitates new beta-lactam agents with activity against ESBL- and AmpC-producing organisms. Cefronexine is a fourth-generation cephalosporin combined with a novel beta-lactamase inhibitor with enhanced spectrum against KPC carbapenemases.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** Demonstrate non-inferiority of cefronexine to ceftriaxone for clinical and microbiologic cure.

**Primary Endpoint:** Composite clinical cure and microbiologic eradication at the test-of-cure (TOC) visit (Day 21).

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- Early clinical response at day 4
- Sustained clinical cure at late follow-up (Day 35)
- All-cause mortality through Day 35

## 3. Study Design

Multicenter, randomized 1:1, double-blind, double-dummy, active-controlled, parallel-group trial. Subjects (N=692) stratified by region and presence of bacteremia.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Hospitalized adults aged 18-90; clinical signs of cUTI; pyuria on urinalysis; one complicating factor (indwelling catheter, urinary obstruction, recent instrumentation, neurogenic bladder, or pyelonephritis).

**Exclusion:** Prior antibiotic effective against the likely pathogen for >24 hours within the prior 72 hours; renal abscess requiring drainage; hemodialysis-dependent renal failure.

## 5. Adverse Events

Standard cephalosporin class safety profile:

- **Diarrhea** — 13%, mild to moderate; C. difficile testing required if persistent.
- **Nausea** — 9%, mild.
- **Headache** — 8%, mild.
- **Constipation** — 6%, mild.
- **Rash** — 4%, mild; one Grade 3 hypersensitivity anticipated per 250 subjects.
- **Pruritus** — 3%.
- **Elevated ALT** — 5%, Grade 1-2.
- **Elevated AST** — 4%, Grade 1-2.
- **Phlebitis at infusion site** — 7%, mild.
- **Pseudomembranous colitis (C. difficile)** — 1.4%, moderate to severe.
- **Eosinophilia** — 3%, asymptomatic.
- **Anemia** — 2%.
- **Thrombocytopenia** — 1%, mild.

Beta-lactam allergy screening required. ID-specialist consultation for any subject with new-onset rash and eosinophilia.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

N=692 provides 90% power to demonstrate non-inferiority with a 10% margin on the composite primary endpoint, assuming an 80% response rate in both arms.

## 7. Monitoring

DSMB review every 12 weeks. Adjudication committee for clinical cure determination.
