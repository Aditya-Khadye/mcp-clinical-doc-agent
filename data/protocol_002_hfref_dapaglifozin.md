# A Phase 2b, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Trial of Empaglozin in Patients With Heart Failure With Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF)

**Protocol Number:** CV-2026-007
**Sponsor:** Meridian Cardiovascular Therapeutics
**IND Number:** 162,901
**Phase:** Phase 2b
**Indication:** Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF)
**Intervention:** Empaglozin 10 mg orally once daily for 24 weeks versus matching placebo, added to standard guideline-directed medical therapy.

## 1. Background

SGLT2 inhibitors have demonstrated mortality benefit in HFrEF independent of diabetes status. Empaglozin is a novel selective SGLT2 inhibitor with extended half-life designed to improve once-daily exposure consistency.

## 2. Objectives

**Primary Objective:** Evaluate the effect of empaglozin on NT-proBNP levels at week 24.

**Primary Endpoint:** Change from baseline in NT-proBNP at week 24.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- 6-minute walk distance
- Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire score
- Incidence of heart failure hospitalization

## 3. Study Design

A multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group trial. Subjects (N=312) will be randomized 1:1 to empaglozin or placebo. Stratification factors: baseline NT-proBNP, NYHA class, and diabetes status.

## 4. Eligibility

**Inclusion:** Adults ≥18 years; symptomatic HFrEF with LVEF ≤40%; NYHA class II-IV; stable on guideline-directed medical therapy for ≥4 weeks.

**Exclusion:** eGFR <25 mL/min/1.73m²; recent type 1 diabetes-related DKA; recent acute coronary syndrome.

## 5. Adverse Events

Expected safety profile based on the SGLT2 inhibitor class:

- **Urinary tract infection** — 9% (vs 6% placebo), predominantly mild.
- **Genital mycotic infection** — 5%, mild severity.
- **Hypotension** — 7%, generally Grade 1-2; symptomatic in 2%.
- **Hypoglycemia** — 4% in non-diabetic subjects, mild; up to 11% in subjects on background sulfonylurea.
- **Dizziness** — 6%, mild.
- **Dehydration / volume depletion** — 4%, moderate severity in elderly.
- **Acute kidney injury** — 3%, typically transient; reversible upon drug interruption.
- **Diabetic ketoacidosis** — rare (<0.5%) but Grade 3-4 when it occurs.
- **Constipation** — 3%.

A separate cardiovascular adjudication committee will review all suspected MACE events.

## 6. Statistical Considerations

The primary analysis will use mixed-model repeated measures (MMRM). N=312 provides 85% power to detect a 20% relative reduction in NT-proBNP.

## 7. Monitoring

Interim analysis for futility at 50% enrollment. DSMB review every 12 weeks.
